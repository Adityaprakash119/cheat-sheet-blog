Reasons NOT To Cheat

Cheating doesn’t typically happen in isolation. It’s a bad habit to start at any point in time. There are so many reasons not to cheat. The biggest reason of all would be because of the consequences that may include: receiving a zero, failing a class, being suspended, or being expelled.

Additionally, cheating creates a culture of mistrust, leads people to adopt a mentality that cuts corners, and devalues education on behalf of both students and teachers.




Alternatives to Cheating

Thinking of cheating? Before you make this serious mistake, consider these alternatives to cheating that will support your education and allow you to absorb knowledge:


  • Study hard
  • Practice time management
  • Ask for help
  • Get a tutor
  • Take practice tests
  • Don’t give up!


The Cold Hard Truth


Cheating on a test is taking the easy way out that becomes the hard way. The entire point of education and tests is to see that you actually understand the concepts you’ve been taught. The worst-case scenario of not cheating and not doing well is potentially failing. If that’s the case, you will be able to see where your weaknesses reside and get help to resolve your pain points.

All institutions of education hold a zero-tolerance policy when it comes to cheating, including the University of the People – a tuition-free, US accredited, online university. Online or not, cheating is a real offense with real consequences. At the end of the day, wouldn’t you rather walk away from a course with both integrity and knowledge?
